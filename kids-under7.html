Staying safe online 🌟

Simple rules to keep you happy and safe when you're on a tablet, phone or computer.

Learn these off by heart

The 4 big rules

🧑‍🤝‍🧑

Only talk to people you know in real life

If someone messages you and you don't know them from school or family, tell a grown-up straight away. Don't reply.

✋

Always ask a grown-up first

Before you watch something new, download a game, or click on a link — check with a trusted adult first.

🔒

Keep your details private

Never tell anyone your full name, address, school, or where you live online — even in games.

❤️

Tell a grown-up if something feels wrong

If you see something that makes you feel sad, scared or confused — you won't be in trouble. Tell someone straight away.

The digital PANTS rule

P Private

Some things about you are private — like your name, address and school.

A Always ask

Always ask a grown-up before going somewhere new online.

N No means no

If something online makes you feel uncomfortable, it's OK to say no and leave.

T Tell someone

Always tell a trusted adult if something feels wrong — you won't be in trouble.

S Secrets

Never keep secrets online. Grown-ups who love you want to know.

💛 Nothing bad that happens online is ever your fault

If something scary happened, a grown-up who loves you will want to help. You will not be in trouble for telling them. They will be glad you told them.

📋 For parents reading this together

This page is designed to be read together with your child. The PANTS rule connects to the NSPCC's body safety framework most UK schools already teach — making the digital link helps reinforce what they already know.
