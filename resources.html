Trusted organisations

Further resources from UK safety bodies

CEOP & Thinkuknow

Police-led educational resources on online safety, grooming, and how to report exploitation.

ceopeducation.co.uk — resources for educators and parents ceop.police.uk — report online exploitation now

NSPCC

The UK's leading child protection charity — online safety guides, research, and a professional helpline.

nspcc.org.uk — online safety hub 0808 800 5000 — helpline for adults worried about a child

Childline

Free, confidential support for children and young people — 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.

childline.org.uk — chat online anytime 0800 1111 — free, 24/7, no need to give your name

Internet Matters

Platform-specific guides, parental control walkthroughs, and age-appropriate advice for families.

internetmatters.org — guides by age and platform Parental controls guide — every device

Revenge Porn Helpline

Free specialist support for anyone whose intimate images have been shared without consent. Image removal service.

revengepornhelpline.org.uk 0345 600 0459 — Mon–Fri 10am–4pm

Internet Watch Foundation

Report illegal content online — including sexual images of children. Works to remove content from the internet.

iwf.org.uk/report — report illegal content iwf.org.uk — about the IWF

Young Minds

Mental health support for children and young people — including resources for online-related anxiety and distress.

youngminds.org.uk Social media and mental health guide

National Centre for Gaming Disorders

NHS-funded therapy for children and adults experiencing gaming addiction or related mental health issues.

cnwl.nhs.uk — gaming disorders
