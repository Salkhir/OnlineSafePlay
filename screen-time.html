Screen time & devices

NHS-backed time limits by age, parental controls for every device, sleep science, and how to set up phone-free zones without constant conflict.

Controls 6 min read Updated June 2026
Evidence-based

Recommended daily limits by age

Based on NHS, WHO and American Academy of Pediatrics guidance. "Screen time" means recreational use — video calls with family and educational use are not included.

Under 2

None

No recreational screen time. Video calls with family are the only exception.

2–5

1 hour

High-quality content only, with a parent present. No screens in the hour before bed.

6–12

2 hours

Consistent limits. Physical activity, sleep and homework should not be displaced by screens.

13–17

2–3 hours

Quality matters as much as quantity. Social connection and sleep should not be sacrificed.

Source: NHS, WHO and American Academy of Pediatrics guidance. These are recommendations, not hard rules — context and content type matter significantly.

Step by step

Parental controls — every device

📱 iPhone & iPad — Screen Time

1Settings → Screen Time → Turn On Screen Time
2Set a Screen Time passcode (different from device PIN)
3Downtime: set hours when only allowed apps work (e.g. 9pm–7am)
4App Limits: set daily limits per category (Social Networking, Games)
5Communication Limits: restrict calls and messages to contacts only
6Content & Privacy: block explicit content, restrict app downloads

🤖 Android — Digital Wellbeing & Family Link

1Install Google Family Link on your phone and your child's device
2Link accounts — you manage from your phone remotely
3Set daily screen time limits and bedtime lock schedules
4Approve or block app downloads from Google Play
5View location and activity reports
6Set content filters on Google Search and YouTube

🎮 Xbox — Family Safety app

1Download Xbox Family Safety app on your phone
2Create a child Microsoft account and link it to your family
3Set daily play time limits and schedules
4Require approval for all purchases — set a spending limit
5Communication: set to "Friends" only — blocks stranger contact
6Content filters: set PEGI age rating limit

🎮 PlayStation — Family Management

1Create a Family on PlayStation Network via account.sonyentertainmentnetwork.com
2Add child account — set their date of birth accurately
3Set monthly spending limit (including zero)
4Set content restriction level by age rating
5Restrict communication with non-friends
6Set monthly play time reports to be sent to your email

📱 Screens and sleep — the science

71%of teenagers sleep with their phone in their bedroom, according to Ofcom research
1–2hrsaverage sleep lost per night by children who use screens in the hour before bed
3 dayshow quickly sleep quality improves after removing devices from the bedroom, in most studies

Blue light from screens suppresses melatonin production — the hormone that triggers sleep. But the bigger factor is cognitive stimulation: social media notifications, game tension, and messaging all keep the brain alert when it needs to wind down. The recommendation from NHS and sleep specialists is no screens of any kind in the 60 minutes before sleep, and devices charged outside the bedroom.

Family habits

Phone-free zones — the three highest impact

🛏️

Bedrooms

The most impactful change. Devices charged in a common area overnight. Removes temptation entirely and improves sleep quality within days.

🍽️

Mealtimes

All screens off during meals — including parents'. Research links family mealtimes without phones to better mental health outcomes and stronger parent-child relationships.

🌅

First 30 minutes of the day

No screens before breakfast. Starting the day without the stimulation of notifications and social media reduces anxiety and improves focus throughout the day.

Making it stick

How to implement limits without constant conflict

Make it a family rule, not a child rule. "No phones at the dinner table" applies to everyone — including parents. Children are far more likely to accept rules they see adults following.

Put limits in writing before they're needed. Use our family tech contract builder to create a written agreement while things are calm — not as a reaction to a problem. Rules agreed in advance feel less like punishment.

Use technology to enforce technology limits. Parental controls on devices remove the daily negotiation. "The phone turns off at 9pm" is more sustainable than "you need to put it down now" every night.

Give a 10-minute warning. Abruptly removing a device mid-game or mid-conversation causes disproportionate distress. A timer or verbal warning gives children a chance to reach a natural stopping point.

Review limits every 6 months. A rule that works for a 10-year-old may not work for a 13-year-old. Regular, calm reviews where your child's input is genuinely considered maintain trust and cooperation.
