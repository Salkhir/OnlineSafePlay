Good to know

Things worth remembering

You don't know who's really there

In games, someone might say they're 11 when they're actually an adult. You can't tell from a username or avatar. That's not your fault — it's just how the internet works. Always tell a trusted adult if something feels weird.

The screenshot test

Before you send any message or photo, ask yourself: "Would I be OK if my teacher or parent saw this?" If the answer is no — don't send it. Screenshots travel fast, even in "private" chats.

Reporting isn't snitching

Every game and app has a report button. Using it helps make that space safer for everyone — including you and your friends. You won't get in trouble for reporting someone who made you feel uncomfortable.

Nothing online is ever your fault

If someone was horrible to you, or you accidentally saw something upsetting, that is never your fault. The only thing that matters is telling someone you trust so it can stop.

If something goes wrong

Do these 4 things

Step 1

Stop

Don't reply or send anything else. Put the device down for a moment.

Step 2

Screenshot it

Take a screenshot so there's a record of what happened.

Step 3

Block and report

Use the report button in the app, then block the person.

Step 4

Tell a trusted adult

Show them the screenshot. You will not be in trouble.
